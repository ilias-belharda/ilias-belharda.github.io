Reverse Engineering ‚Äì Exploit Forum Analysis (Dark Web)

Context

This academic project focuses on the analysis and reverse engineering of exploit forums hosted on the dark web, commonly used for selling and sharing attack tools and vulnerabilities.

Objectives

  • Understand how exploit forums operate
  • Identify the types of content and services offered
  • Analyze associated security risks
  • Study obfuscation and concealment techniques

Methodology

  • Selection of exploit forums accessible via anonymous networks
  • Deployment of a secure analysis environment (sandbox)
  • Static analysis of content and structures
  • Dynamic analysis of suspicious behaviors
  • Extraction of Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

Tools Used

Virtualized environments, sandboxing, network analysis, monitoring tools, reverse engineering utilities, and isolated browsers.

Results

Identification of potential threats, risk classification, understanding of criminal business models, and development of prevention and detection recommendations.

Added Value

This project strengthens skills in advanced threat analysis, reverse engineering, cyber investigation, and defensive approaches against dark web criminal ecosystems.
